Surah Al-Baqarah

Verse 2:255
اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ

Allah! There is no deity save Him, the Alive, the Eternal.

The Merits of Ayat al-Kursi

This verse is widely regarded as the greatest verse in the Book of Allah. It was reported in the Sahih that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ affirmed its status because it contains the Ism Allah al-A'zam (the Greatest Name of Allah).

The linguistic composition of the verse begins with the divine name, followed by a declaration of His absolute oneness and eternal existence. The attributes Al-Hayy (The Ever-Living) and Al-Qayyum (The Sustainer of all existence) encompass all other divine attributes of perfection.

Reciting this verse brings immense protection. It acts as a fortress for the believer against spiritual harm. Scholars have noted that the rhythm and flow of the verse in Arabic carry a unique weight, balancing majesty with mercy, and power with proximity.

When one contemplates "Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep," it instills a profound sense of comfort. The Guardian who watches over the universe never tires, ensuring that not a single atom moves without His knowledge and permission. This negation of weakness serves to affirm His absolute perfection in life and sustainment.

Source: Tafsir Ibn Kathir (Abridged)
